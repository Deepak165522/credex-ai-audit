# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Target User

The primary target user is:

* Engineering managers at early-stage startups (5–50 employees)
* Technical founders running AI-heavy workflows
* Startup operators managing rising AI subscription costs
* Indie hackers and developer teams using multiple AI tools simultaneously

These users are highly sensitive to recurring SaaS costs but often lack visibility into whether their AI stack is financially optimized.

The ideal customer profile is a startup spending between $300 and $5,000 per month on AI infrastructure and productivity tooling.

---

# User Intent and Discovery Timing

Users are most likely to want this product when:

* Reviewing monthly SaaS expenses
* Scaling engineering teams
* Comparing AI coding assistants
* Looking for ways to reduce startup burn
* Evaluating whether enterprise AI plans are worth the cost

Typical search intent examples include:

* “Best ChatGPT alternative for teams”
* “Reduce AI API costs”
* “Cursor vs Copilot pricing”
* “AI tooling for startups”
* “How to optimize SaaS spend”

The product is designed to intercept users during moments of financial review and tooling evaluation.

# How I Would Get The First 100 Users

The first 100 users would come from highly targeted founder and developer communities rather than paid advertising.

Primary acquisition methods:

1. Launch a Product Hunt post focused on “Stop Overpaying For AI Tools”

2. Share real savings screenshots on X/Twitter with transparent examples

3. Post comparison threads in relevant subreddits discussing AI tooling costs

4. Create short LinkedIn posts targeting startup founders and engineering managers

5. Share a free AI spend calculator in Indie Hackers communities

6. Reach out directly to founders using AI-heavy workflows and offer free audits

7. Publish a blog post comparing real-world AI tooling stacks and pricing inefficiencies

The focus would be on distribution through credibility and useful financial insights rather than aggressive marketing.

# Unfair Distribution Advantage

Credex has a unique advantage because it directly operates in the AI infrastructure credits market.

Most competitors can identify overspending, but Credex can also provide an actionable financial solution by helping startups purchase discounted AI credits.

This creates a stronger conversion loop:

* Discover overspending
* Recommend optimizations
* Offer discounted credits through Credex

The audit tool becomes both:

* A free utility product
* A lead-generation engine

# What Success Looks Like In Week 1

If the launch is successful, realistic early traction targets would include:

* 2,000+ landing page visitors
* 300+ completed audits
* 75+ captured leads
* 15+ consultation requests
* 3–5 paying credit customers

The most important signal would not be raw traffic, but the percentage of users who:

* Complete audits
* Share reports publicly
* Request consultation follow-ups

Strong organic sharing would indicate real product-market resonance.
