# ECONOMICS.md

# Unit Economics Analysis

## Overview

The AI Spend Audit platform functions as a lead-generation engine for Credex’s discounted AI infrastructure credits marketplace.

The core business model is:

1. Help startups discover unnecessary AI spending
2. Generate trust through actionable savings recommendations
3. Convert high-savings users into Credex consultation leads
4. Monetize through discounted AI credit purchases

---

# Estimated Customer Value

The ideal customer is a startup spending between $500 and $5,000 per month on AI infrastructure and productivity tooling.

Assumptions:

* Average customer monthly AI spend: $2,000
* Average savings opportunity identified: 20%
* Credex captures a margin by facilitating discounted credits
* Estimated annual revenue per converted customer: $3,000–$8,000

For this analysis, a conservative average customer value of:

* $5,000 ARR per converted customer

is assumed.

---

# Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

## Organic Community Distribution

Channels:

* Product Hunt
* Hacker News
* Reddit
* Indie Hackers
* X/Twitter founder communities

Estimated CAC:

* $0–$15 per qualified lead

This works because the product naturally generates curiosity and shareable savings screenshots.

---

## Founder Outreach

Direct outreach to startup founders and engineering managers.

Estimated CAC:

* $25–$60 per qualified lead

This channel is slower but produces higher-intent users.

---

## Content Marketing

Blog posts and AI tooling comparison content.

Estimated CAC:

* $40–$120 per converted customer over time

Higher upfront effort, but scalable long-term.

---

# Conversion Funnel Assumptions

Example funnel:

* 1,000 visitors
* 250 completed audits (25%)
* 75 lead captures (30%)
* 15 consultation bookings (20%)
* 3 paying customers (20%)

If:

* 3 customers × $5,000 ARR

Then:

* $15,000 ARR generated from 1,000 visitors

This implies the funnel can become highly profitable if audit completion and lead conversion remain strong.

---

# Profitability Requirements

The platform becomes economically attractive if:

* Audit completion rate stays above 20%
* Lead capture rate exceeds 25%
* Consultation booking rate exceeds 15%
* Customer conversion rate remains above 10%

Because distribution channels are largely organic and content-driven, the economics improve significantly with scale.

---

# Path To $1M ARR

Assumptions:

* Average customer value: $5,000 ARR
* Required customers for $1M ARR:

  * 200 active customers

Example acquisition model:

* 100,000 annual visitors
* 20% audit completion rate
* 25% lead capture rate
* 10% paid conversion rate

Results:

* 20,000 audits
* 5,000 leads
* 500 customers

Even with conservative retention assumptions, this comfortably exceeds $1M ARR potential.

---

# Key Risks

Potential risks include:

* AI pricing changes reducing optimization opportunities
* Low user trust in financial recommendations
* Weak conversion from free audit to consultation
* Platform becoming too dependent on organic distribution

To mitigate these risks, Credex would need:

* Accurate pricing data
* Strong case studies
* Credible savings calculations
* Transparent financial reasoning

---

# Why This Model Works

Most startups already spend heavily on AI infrastructure but lack visibility into whether their tooling decisions are financially efficient.

The audit product creates value before asking for anything in return:

* Immediate savings insights
* Actionable recommendations
* Transparent reasoning

This creates trust, which is critical for converting users into infrastructure credit customers later.
