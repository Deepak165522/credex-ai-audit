# Product Metrics Strategy

## North Star Metric

The primary North Star metric for the platform is:

### Qualified Audit Completions Per Week

A “qualified audit” means:

* A user completes the audit flow
* The reported monthly AI spend exceeds a meaningful threshold
* The user views the recommendations page
* The user demonstrates genuine purchasing potential

This metric matters more than raw traffic because the business goal is not maximizing visitors — it is identifying startups with real AI infrastructure spending problems.

---

# Input Metrics

## 1. Audit Completion Rate

Definition:

* Percentage of landing page visitors who complete the audit process

Why it matters:

* Indicates whether the onboarding flow is understandable
* Measures how compelling the product positioning is
* Helps identify friction in the form experience

Target:

* 20–30% completion rate

---

## 2. Lead Capture Rate

Definition:

* Percentage of completed audits that result in an email submission

Why it matters:

* Measures whether users perceive enough value in the audit results
* Indicates trust and perceived usefulness

Target:

* 25–40% lead capture rate

---

## 3. Consultation Booking Rate

Definition:

* Percentage of high-savings users who request follow-up consultation

Why it matters:

* Directly measures commercial intent
* Strongest signal for monetization potential

Target:

* 10–20% of high-savings audits

---

# What I Would Instrument First

The first analytics events I would track:

* Landing page visits
* Audit started
* Audit completed
* Savings amount generated
* Email submitted
* PDF exported
* Shareable report generated
* Consultation CTA clicked

This instrumentation would help identify:

* Funnel drop-off points
* Most valuable acquisition channels
* Which savings thresholds convert best

---

# Early Product Success Signals

The strongest early signal would be:

* Users voluntarily sharing audit results publicly

This indicates:

* The audit output feels credible
* The product creates emotional impact
* The results are socially valuable

Strong shareability significantly lowers future acquisition costs.

---

# Pivot Threshold

A pivot decision would be considered if:

* Audit completion rate remains below 10%
* Lead capture rate remains below 10%
* Users consistently report unrealistic recommendations
* High-savings users do not convert into consultations

This would suggest either:

* Weak positioning
* Poor recommendation quality
* Insufficient trust in the audit output

At that point, the product would likely need:

* Better financial benchmarking
* More credible data sources
* Stronger personalization
* Simpler onboarding flow

---

# Long-Term Metrics Expansion

As the platform scales, additional metrics would include:

* Average savings identified per audit
* Revenue generated per acquisition channel
* Customer lifetime value
* Referral conversion rate
* Repeat audit frequency
* Shareable report engagement rate

The long-term goal would be building a trusted AI spend optimization platform rather than simply maximizing traffic.
